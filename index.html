Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our services

Installation

The duration of an elevator installation depends on several factors including the type of elevator, building structure, and customization requirements. On average, a standard passenger elevator installation takes between 8-12 weeks from start to completion. This includes planning, construction, installation, and testing phases.

Space requirements vary based on the elevator type. For a standard hydraulic elevator, you'll typically need a minimum pit depth of 4-5 feet and an overhead clearance of about 10-12 feet. Machine room-less (MRL) elevators require less space as they don't need a separate machine room. Our team can assess your space and recommend the best solution.

Maintenance

We recommend quarterly maintenance (4 times per year) for most standard elevators. However, high-traffic elevators in commercial buildings may require monthly maintenance. Our service team will assess your specific needs and recommend an appropriate maintenance schedule during your initial consultation.

Our standard maintenance contract includes regular inspections, lubrication of moving parts, adjustment of door operators, testing of safety features, cleaning of critical components, and 24/7 emergency call support. We also provide detailed service reports after each visit and priority scheduling for any necessary repairs.

Modernization

Signs that your elevator may need modernization include frequent breakdowns, increased energy consumption, outdated technology, difficulty getting parts, non-compliance with current safety codes, or if it's more than 15-20 years old. Our experts can conduct a comprehensive assessment to determine if modernization would be beneficial.

Modernizing your elevator can provide numerous benefits including improved reliability, enhanced safety features, better energy efficiency, reduced maintenance costs, increased property value, compliance with current regulations, and improved ride comfort. Modernization is often more cost-effective than complete replacement.

Safety & Compliance

All our elevators comply with the latest safety standards including EN 81-20/50, ASME A17.1/CSA B44, and local building codes. We ensure that every installation meets or exceeds these standards through rigorous testing and quality control measures. Our team stays current with all regulatory changes to ensure continuous compliance.

If you're inside the elevator when it stops, stay calm and use the emergency phone or alarm button. Do not attempt to force the doors open. If you're outside and someone is trapped, reassure them that help is on the way and call our 24/7 emergency service line immediately. Our team typically responds within 60 minutes for emergency calls.
